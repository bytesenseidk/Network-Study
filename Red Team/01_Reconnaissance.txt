Reconnaissance
- The process of gathering information about targets, infrastructure, and potential attack paths before attempting exploitation.

Overview:
- Reconnaissance is the first phase of an attack or penetration test.
- During this stage, attackers collect as much information as possible bout a target organization, its systems, networks, and technologies.
- This information helps identify potential weaknesses and determine the most effective attack methods.

Passive Reconnaissance:
- Passive reconnaissance gathers information about a target without directly interacting with the target systems.
- Because it does not generate trafic toward the target network, it is difficult to detect.

Open Source Intelligence (OSINT):
- OSINT refers to information collected from publicly available sources such as websites, social media, search engines, public records, and leaked data repositories.
- Attackers use OSINT to discover employee names, technologies used by the organization, email formats, and other usefull information.


DNS Enumeration::
- DNS enumeration involves collecting information from Domain Name System records.
- These records can reveal subdomains, mail servers, name servers, and infrastructure used by an organization.
- This information helps attackers map external services and potential entry points.

Domain Intelligence:
- Domain intelligence focuses on analyzing domain ownership, registration details, and infrastructure relationships.
- Tools and public databases can reveal domain registration information, associated domains, and hosting providers.

Metadata Collection:
- Files published online may contain hidden metadata such as author names, software versions, internal usernames, and system paths.
- Attackers can extract this metadata from documents, images, and PDFs to gain insights into the internal environment.

Active Reconnaissance:
- Active reconnaissance involves directly interacting with target systems to gather technical information.
- Because it generates network traffic, it can potentially be detected by monitoring systems.

Network Scanning:
- Network scanning identifies systems that are reachable on a network.
- Attackers send probes to determine which hosts are active and repsonding.

Host Discovery:
- Host discovery identifies which devices are currently active on a network.
- Techniques may include ICMP requests, ARP scanning, or TCP probes to determine live systems.

Port Scanning:
- Port scanning identifies open ports on target systems.
- Techniques may include ICMP requests, ARP scanning, or TCP probes to determine live systems.

Port Scanning:
- Port scanning identifies open ports on target systems.
- Open ports indicate services that may be running and potentially vulnerable to attack.

Service Enumeration:
- Service enumeration gathers detailed information about services running on open ports.
- Attackers attempt to identify the service type, configuration, and potential vulnerabilities.

Version Detection:
- Version detection attempts to identify the specific version of software running on a service.
- Knowing the exact version allows attackers to search for known vulnerabilities or exploits targeting that software.


Purpose of Reconnaissance:
- The goal of reconnaissance is to reduce uncertenty about the target environment.
- By gathering information about systems, services, and technologies, attackers can identify weak points and plan effective attack strategies.

Example Reconnaissance Workflow:
1. Collect public information about the organization.
2. Identify domains and subdomains.
3. Discover externally accessible hosts.
4. Scan for open ports.
5. Identify running services.
6. Determine software versions.
7. Search for known vulnerabilities.
