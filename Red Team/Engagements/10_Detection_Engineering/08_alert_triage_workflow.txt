Alert Triage Workflow
- The structured process used by security analysts to evaluate, prioritize, and respond to alerts generated by detection systems.

==================================================
1. Overview
==================================================
Alert triage is the process of analyzing security alerts to determine:

- whether the alert is valid
- the severity of the threat
- the appropriate response

Triage is a critical SOC function that ensures:

- real threats are handled quickly
- false positives are filtered out
- analyst time is used efficiently

Without proper triage, security teams may miss real attacks due to alert fatigue.


==================================================
2. Objectives
==================================================

Validate Alerts

Determine if the alert represents malicious activity.

Prioritize Incidents

Assess severity and impact.

Reduce Noise

Filter out false positives.

Initiate Response

Trigger appropriate incident response actions.

Document Findings

Ensure traceability and knowledge sharing.


==================================================
3. Alert Triage Workflow
==================================================

Alert Triggered
        ↓
Initial Review
        ↓
Context Enrichment
        ↓
Validation (True/False Positive)
        ↓
Severity Classification
        ↓
Escalation or Closure
        ↓
Documentation


==================================================
4. Alert Severity Levels
==================================================

Informational

No immediate threat, low priority.

Low

Suspicious but unlikely to be malicious.

Medium

Potentially malicious activity.

High

Likely malicious behavior.

Critical

Confirmed or high-impact compromise.


==================================================
5. Step-by-Step Triage Process
==================================================

Step 1 — Initial Review

Examine alert details:

- alert type
- timestamp
- affected user/system


Step 2 — Context Enrichment

Gather additional data:

- user activity
- host information
- IP reputation
- geolocation


Step 3 — Validate Alert

Determine:

- true positive
- false positive


Step 4 — Investigate Further (if needed)

Analyze:

- related logs
- historical activity
- process execution
- network connections


Step 5 — Assign Severity

Based on:

- impact
- scope
- confidence


Step 6 — Take Action

Options:

- escalate to incident response
- block user/IP
- isolate host
- close alert


Step 7 — Document Findings

Record:

- analysis performed
- outcome
- actions taken


==================================================
6. Example Triage Scenario
==================================================

Alert:

Multiple failed login attempts detected.


Initial Review:

User: john.doe@example.com  
Source IP: 192.168.1.50  


Context Enrichment:

- IP location: foreign country  
- multiple users targeted  


Validation:

Likely password spraying attack.


Severity:

High


Action:

- block IP
- reset affected accounts
- escalate incident

==================================================
8. Common Triage Decisions
==================================================

False Positive

No action required.

Benign Activity

Expected system or user behavior.

Suspicious Activity

Requires monitoring.

Confirmed Incident

Requires immediate response.

Escalation

Hand off to incident response team.


==================================================
9. Common Mistakes
==================================================

Rushing Analysis

Missing key context.

Ignoring Baseline Behavior

Misclassifying normal activity.

Poor Documentation

Lack of audit trail.

Overlooking Correlation

Failing to connect related events.

Alert Fatigue

Ignoring alerts due to volume.


==================================================
10. Best Practices
==================================================

Follow a Structured Workflow

Ensure consistency across analysts.

Use Context Enrichment

Always gather additional data.

Prioritize Effectively

Focus on high-risk alerts.

Document Everything

Maintain detailed records.

Collaborate with Teams

Share insights across SOC.


==================================================
11. Example Triage Checklist
==================================================

- What triggered the alert?
- Which user/system is affected?
- Is the activity expected?
- Are there related events?
- What is the potential impact?
- What action should be taken?

13. Questions & Answers
==================================================

Question:
What is alert triage?

Answer:
The process of analyzing and prioritizing security alerts.

--------------------------------------------------

Question:
Why is alert triage important?

Answer:
It ensures real threats are identified and handled efficiently.

--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What is a false positive?

Answer:
A benign activity incorrectly flagged as malicious.

--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What is context enrichment?

Answer:
Gathering additional data to better understand an alert.

--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What is the goal of alert triage?

Answer:
To determine the validity and severity of alerts and initiate appropriate response.
