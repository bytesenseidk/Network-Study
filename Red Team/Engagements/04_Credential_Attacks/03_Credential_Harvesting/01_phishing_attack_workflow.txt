1. Overview
==================================================
Phishing attacks attempt to deceive users into providing sensitive information such as:

- usernames
- passwords
- authentication tokens
- financial data

Attackers typically impersonate trusted organizations or individuals.

Common delivery methods include:

- email messages
- messaging platforms
- fake login portals
- malicious attachments
- spoofed websites

Phishing remains one of the most successful attack techniques because it targets human behavior rather than technical vulnerabilities.


==================================================
2. Attack Scenario
==================================================
Assume an attacker targets employees of an organization.

The attacker prepares a phishing campaign designed to impersonate a legitimate service such as:

- Microsoft 365
- corporate VPN
- internal HR portal
- cloud storage service

Victims receive a message directing them to a fake login page.

When users enter their credentials, the attacker captures the information.

5. Step-by-Step Execution
==================================================

Step 1 — Reconnaissance

Gather information about the target organization.

Examples include:

- employee names
- email formats
- commonly used services
- internal terminology

This information helps craft convincing phishing messages.


Step 2 — Create Phishing Infrastructure

Prepare phishing components such as:

- fake login page
- spoofed domain name
- email sending infrastructure

Example phishing domain:

secure-company-login.com


Step 3 — Craft Phishing Message

Create a message designed to prompt immediate action.

Examples:

- password reset notification
- security alert
- invoice request
- document sharing notification


Step 4 — Deliver Phishing Emails

Send the phishing message to targeted users.

Example email content:

"Your account password has expired. Please log in to update your credentials."


Step 5 — Capture Credentials

Victims visit the phishing page and enter their login information.

Captured data may include:

- username
- password
- authentication tokens


Step 6 — Use Captured Credentials

Attackers attempt to authenticate to the real service using the captured credentials.

Example:

VPN login  
Cloud service access  
Email account access

==================================================
7. Expected Results
==================================================
Successful phishing attacks may result in:

- compromised user accounts
- unauthorized access to internal systems
- email account compromise
- credential reuse across services
- expanded attacker access within the organization


==================================================
8. Defensive Considerations
==================================================
Organizations should implement multiple defenses against phishing attacks.

User Awareness Training

Educate employees on identifying suspicious emails and links.

Email Security Filtering

Use email filtering solutions to detect phishing attempts.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Require MFA to prevent account compromise even if passwords are stolen.

Domain Monitoring

Monitor for domains that imitate the organization's brand.

Incident Monitoring

Investigate unusual login patterns or authentication attempts.

10. Blue Team Detection
==================================================
Phishing attacks typically involve malicious email delivery, credential harvesting, and abnormal authentication activity.

Common indicators include:

- emails containing suspicious links or attachments
- messages originating from spoofed domains
- login attempts shortly after a phishing email was opened
- multiple users receiving identical phishing messages
- authentication attempts from unusual geographic locations

Additional indicators may include:

- newly registered domains mimicking legitimate services
- email forwarding rules created after account compromise
- abnormal OAuth or application authorization requests.

13. Mitigation Strategies
==================================================

Security Awareness Training

Regularly train employees to identify phishing attempts.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Reduce risk of account compromise.

Email Filtering

Block malicious attachments and links.

Domain Monitoring

Detect domains impersonating the organization.

Conditional Access Policies

Incident Response Procedures

Rapidly investigate reported phishing emails.

What is a phishing attack?

Answer:
A social engineering attack that tricks users into revealing sensitive information such as credentials.

Because they exploit human trust and urgency rather than technical vulnerabilities.

--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What information do phishing attacks often target?

Answer:
Usernames, passwords, authentication tokens, and financial information.
