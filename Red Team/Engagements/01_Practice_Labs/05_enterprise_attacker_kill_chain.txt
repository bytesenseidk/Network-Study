Enterprise Attacker Kill Chain
- A high-level attack model describing how attackers progress from initial access to full domain compromise in enterprise environments.

==================================================
Overview
==================================================
Enterprise breaches typically follow a structured progression where attackers gradually increase their level of access.

This process is often referred to as an **attacker kill chain**.

The kill chain describes how attackers move through stages such as:

- reconnaissance
- initial access
- credential theft
- lateral movement
- domain compromise
- persistence

Understanding the kill chain helps defenders detect attacks earlier and respond effectively.


==================================================
Enterprise Attack Stages
==================================================

1. Reconnaissance
2. Initial Access

4. Internal Discovery
5. Lateral Movement
6. Privilege Escalation
7. Domain Compromise
8. Persistence
9. Data Exfiltration / Impact


==================================================
1. Reconnaissance
==================================================
Attackers gather information about the target organization.

This may include:

- domain names
- employee identities
- email formats
- external infrastructure
- public services

Common techniques include:

OSINT collection  
DNS enumeration  
service scanning  
social media research

Tools may include:

- Nmap
- theHarvester
- Amass
- Recon-ng


==================================================
2. Initial Access
==================================================
Attackers attempt to gain a foothold inside the network.

Common entry methods include:

Phishing attacks  
Credential stuffing  
password spraying  
exposed remote services  
vulnerable web applications  
compromised VPN credentials

4. Internal Discovery
==================================================
Once inside the network, attackers begin mapping the environment.

They identify:

- active hosts
- running services
- domain controllers
- administrative accounts
- trust relationships

6. Privilege Escalation
==================================================
Attackers attempt to obtain higher privileges.

7. Domain Compromise
==================================================
At this stage attackers target Active Directory infrastructure.

Techniques may include:

==================================================
8. Persistence
==================================================
Attackers establish long-term access to the environment.

This ensures access remains even after credentials are changed.

Common techniques include:

Golden Ticket attacks  
Silver Ticket attacks  
backdoor accounts  
scheduled tasks

==================================================
9. Data Exfiltration / Impact
==================================================
In the final stage attackers achieve their objectives.

This may include:

data theft  
financial fraud  
system sabotage  
ransomware deployment

Attackers may:

- exfiltrate sensitive data
- encrypt systems
- destroy infrastructure

==================================================
Defensive Strategy
==================================================
Organizations should implement layered defenses to disrupt the attacker kill chain.

Key defensive controls include:

strong authentication policies  
multi-factor authentication  
network segmentation  
privileged access management  
endpoint detection and response  
SIEM monitoring  
security awareness training

Questions & Answers
==================================================

Question:
What is the attacker kill chain?

Answer:
A model describing how attackers progress from initial access to full system compromise.


--------------------------------------------------

Question:
Why is the kill chain important for defenders?

Answer:
Because it helps defenders detect attacks earlier and break the attack sequence.


--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What stage typically leads to domain compromise?

Answer:
Privilege escalation followed by attacks such as DCSync.


--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What attack technique often provides long-term persistence?

Answer:
Golden Ticket attacks.


--------------------------------------------------

Question:
What is the best defense against the attacker kill chain?

Answer:
Layered security controls that detect and disrupt each stage of the attack.
